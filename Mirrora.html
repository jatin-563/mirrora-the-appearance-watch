What is Mirrora?

Mirrora is a wrist wearable that surfaces a quick, private view of your appearance and gives AI‑powered feedback: outfit suggestions, grooming nudges, posture tips and confidence scoring — all in 10 seconds.

Core features

  • Mirror Mode: quick selfie preview on your wrist screen (privacy-first: frames stored locally unless you opt-in).
  • Appearance AI: outfit fit, color match, grooming and skin‑health cues (non-diagnostic).
  • Motivation & Habits: nudges tied to posture, hydration, and gym streaks to improve appearance over time.
  • Lookbook: Save outfits and rate them. Shareable with friends privately.
  • Low-light & privacy: screen uses ambient sensing, mute mode, and quick blur on shared captures.

Target audience

Young professionals (18–35), content creators, fashion & fitness-conscious users, and anyone who wants quick, private visual feedback before meetings or content shoots.

How Mirrora works — tech overview

On-device camera + edge AI runs lightweight vision models for face/pose detection. Heavier personalization (e.g., style recommendations) runs in the cloud with user's consent using encrypted uploads. Companion phone app manages lookbooks, firmware updates and advanced analytics.

Tech stack (example)

  • Firmware: Zephyr / FreeRTOS, secure boot
  • On-device ML: TensorFlow Lite Micro / MediaPipe
  • Mobile app: React Native / Flutter
  • Cloud: AWS or GCP (inference, user profiles) + KMS for keys
  • Privacy: differential privacy & end-to-end encryption for uploads

Estimated timeline

  1. Month 0–3: Proof of concept, on-device camera pipeline, UI mockups
  2. Month 3–6: Prototype hardware, pilot users, companion app alpha
  3. Month 6–9: Beta hardware & software, manufacturability testing

Business model

Hardware sales + subscription for premium AI personalization, cloud storage, and social features. OEM partnerships for fashion brands and gyms are secondary revenue streams.

Sustainability

Recyclable watch casing, modular replaceable strap, firmware updates to extend device lifetime, and low-power edge inference to minimize cloud usage.
